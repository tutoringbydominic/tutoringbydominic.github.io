About your tutor

Hi There!

I'm a Robotics Engineering undergraduate at WPI (Worcester Polytechnic Institute) with a minor in Mathematics. My coursework and project work sit at the intersection of physics, applied math, and software, which means the subjects I teach aren't abstractions to me. They're tools I use every week in labs and design projects.

Outside of academics, I enjoy biking, snowboarding, and cooking. I love to watch a ton of movies, Formula 1, and soccer.

Why it works for students

Patient. Precise. Student-led.

Flexible Approaches

Struggling with physics or algebra doesn't mean a student isn't smart, it means they haven't been shown the right approach that makes sense to them. Every question is a good question in this context.

Progress you can actually see

Students recieve tools and strategies to track their improvement over time, ensuring they can visualize their growth and stay motivated.

Conversational, not lecture-style

Sessions are dialogues. I ask questions before I explain, to understand exactly where a concept broke down, then we rebuild from that precise point.

Focused on the fundamentals

Sessions will use student's past work, syllabus, and textbook. The goal is genuine understanding that shows up on exams, not pattern-matching that falls apart under new phrasing.
